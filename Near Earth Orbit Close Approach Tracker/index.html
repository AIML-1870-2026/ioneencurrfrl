How to read this data

▾

Palermo Scale — A logarithmic measure of impact risk compared to the background rate of random impacts. Values below −2 are far below background and essentially negligible. Values from −2 to −1 merit routine monitoring. Values from −1 to 0 are elevated and closely watched. Values at or above 0 exceed the background rate — these are extremely rare and represent genuine scientific concern.

Torino Scale — A simpler 0–10 integer scale for public communication. 0 means no hazard. 1–4 merit monitoring at varying urgency. 5–7 are threatening encounters requiring close attention. 8–10 are certain or near-certain impacts. The overwhelming majority of known objects score 0.
